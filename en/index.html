About the Laboratory

The Soft Matter Physics Laboratory at the Faculty of Science, Yamaguchi University conducts research aimed at elucidating the physical mechanisms underlying collective and nonequilibrium phenomena in colloidal and soft matter systems. To achieve a comprehensive understanding of molecular- and mesoscopic-scale structures and dynamics in liquids and solutions, we employ an integrated approach combining experimental techniques, theoretical analysis, and numerical simulations. 　 A central focus of our research is the development and application of soft X-ray emission spectroscopy (XES), which enables direct observation of electronic states and local structures in liquid environments that are difficult to access using conventional methods. By applying this technique to aqueous and molecular systems, we investigate intermolecular interactions, hydration structures, and emergent ordering specific to soft matter and colloidal systems. Through these studies, we aim to clarify universal features of collective motion, phase separation, and nonequilibrium behavior from the perspective of statistical and chemical physics. In addition to advancing fundamental physical understanding, our work contributes to the establishment of experimental methodologies for liquid-state spectroscopy and provides insights relevant to materials science, interfacial science, and related interdisciplinary fields.

Research Topics

Collective Phenomena in Soft Matter

We study self-organization, phase separation, and collective motion in colloidal and soft matter systems. Particular attention is paid to how particle interactions and external fields control the dynamics and structure of these systems.

Statistical Physics of Nonequilibrium Systems

Our research also focuses on novel physical phenomena emerging far from equilibrium. Through effective theoretical descriptions and numerical analysis, we seek universal principles that transcend specific systems.
